Should you mulch roses for winter?

Mounding roses is the building up of soil or mulch around the base of a rose bush and up onto the canes to a height of 6 to 8 inches (15-20 cm.). These mounds of soil or mulch help to keep the rose bush cold once they have gone through some freezing cold days and nights that have caused them to go dormant. When should I mulch roses for winter? If you have many mice around, you may want to skip the mulch materials as they provide a winter home and ready food source in your rose stems.
