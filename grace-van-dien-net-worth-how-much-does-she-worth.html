    Actress and Twitch broadcaster Grace Van Dien was born in the United States in 1996. She is recognized for her roles as Katie Campbell in the NBC drama series The Village and Brooke Osmond in the Netflix adolescent drama series Greenhouse Academy (2017). She also played Sharon Tate in the movie Charlie Says, which was directed by Mary Harron (2018).

    California’s Los Angeles is where Van Dien was born and reared. She is the great-granddaughter of Hollywood Golden Age actor Robert Mitchum and the daughter of actors Casper Van Dien and Carrie Mitchum.

    Catherine Oxenberg, an actress, was once her stepmother. Van Dien has two half-sisters and one older brother. Van Dien has Dutch, Swedish, French, and English ancestry on her father’s side.

    I Married a Princess, a reality television program that aired on the Lifetime Television channel in the US, featured Van Dien and her family in 2005.

    Throughout her childhood, Van Dien appeared in various minor roles in her father’s movies. Van Dien wanted to be a writer before launching her acting career, but she changed her mind after falling in love with acting while filming Sleeping Beauty (2014).

    She had guest leading roles in the television programs Code Black and White Famous between 2015 and 2017, and she made appearances in a number of television movies and independent productions.

    In the first two seasons of the Netflix teen drama series Greenhouse Academy, Van Dien played Brooke Osmond, which was her first significant television role (2017–2019).

    In the biographical movie Charlie Says, which made its world debut at the 75th Venice International Film Festival, she played Sharon Tate in 2018. Van Dien was chosen to play Katie Campbell, the series’ lead character, in the 2019 season of the NBC drama The Village, which was canceled after one season.

    Van Dien appeared as a guest performer in the fourth season of the Netflix series Stranger Things in 2022. She played Hawkins High School cheerleader Chrissy Cunningham, a popular but disturbed person.

    In the same year, Van Dien started live-streaming video games on Twitch, and within three months of launching her channel, she had amassed over 200,000 subscribers.

    In August 2022, she agreed to be represented by United Talent Agency.
