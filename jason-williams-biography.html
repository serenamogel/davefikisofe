    Who is Jason Williams?

    Jason Williams aka Jason Chandler Williams is a known American former professional basketball player. He played as a point guard for twelve seasons in the National Basketball Association (NBA) during the late 1990s and 2000s

    For his unique style of play, he got the nickname, ‘White Chocolate.’

    In which school did Jason Williams attend?

    Jason Williams went to DuPont High School which is in Belle, the United States. Then, he enrolled at Marshall University where he played from 1994 to 1996 for coach Billy Donovan’s Marshall Thundering Herd men’s basketball.

    Jason Williams- Professional Life, Careers

    After getting suspended from the University of Florida, he was drafted by the Sacramento Kings in the seventh overall selection in the 1998 NBA draft.

    Along with Nick Anderson, he was traded to the Vancouver Grizzlies for Brent Price and Mike Bibby by the Kings.

    In exchange for the shooting guard, Eddie Jones, along with his teammate James Posey they were traded on August 2, 2005.

    In the beginning, he made point guard for the Heat in the 2005 to 2006 campaign where he played 59 games in total.

    From 2006 to 2007, he scored 0.9 points and 5.3 assists and was not able to meet his career averages of 11.7 and 6.5. Afterward, he played 67 games with an average point of 8.7 points and 4.6 assists per game in the 2007 to 2008 season.

    Subsequently, Jason Williams signed a one-year contract with the Los Angeles Clippers in the summer of 2008. Because of persistent injuries, he announced his retirement on September 6, 2008, from the NBA after 10 years.

    By making a return to the NBA, he signed the deal with the Orlando Magic on August 19, 2009. He played 82 games for the Magic in the following year, including 18 starts.

    On August 3, 2010, Jason Williams re-signed the deal with the team. Unfortunately, he suffered from arthroscopic knee surgery, even though he played on October 28. On January 26, 2011, he was released from the Orlando Magic.

    On February 7, 2011, he resigned from a two-year deal with the Memphis Grizzlies. However, Jason Williams made the announcement of his second retirement from the NBA on April 18, 2011.
