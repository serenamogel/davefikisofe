    Martin Kratt Biography

    Martin Kratt is an American educational nature show host and actor. He is best known for creating the children’s series Zoboomafoo and Kratts’ Creatures together with his brother Chris Kratt. He graduated with a Bachelor of Science in Zoology from Duke University.

    Martin And Chris Kratt

    Martin and his younger brother Chris Kratt have created several children’s television series including Kratts’ Creatures, Zoboomafoo, and Be the Creature (which aired on CBC and the National Geographic Channel and Knowledge Network), as well as Wild Kratts which airs on TVOKids and PBS Kids.

    He co-hosts all of his educational childhood programs with his brother Chris Kratt. The two are the main characters on the animated TV series Wild Kratts, which airs on PBS Kids. They also appear as themselves in the Odd Squad episode “Night Shift”.

    In 1990, Martin and his brother Chris founded The Earth Creatures Company, which specializes in wildlife entertainment. The two have also authored eight wildlife books for children (including Creatures in Crisis and Where’re the Bears?) published by Scholastic.

    Martin and his brother Chris currently live in Ottawa, Ontario, as of 2018, where their series Wild Kratts is produced.

    Martin Kratt Age | How Old Is Martin Kratt

    He was born Martin William Kratt on December 23, 1965 in Warren Township, New Jersey, United States. He is 52 years old as of 2018.

    Martin Kratt Height

    He stands at a height of 5 feet 8 inches (1.73 m).

    Martin Kratt Family

    He is the grandson of musical-instrument manufacturer William Jacob Kratt, Sr. Martin is the son of William Kratt, Jr. (father) and Linda Kratt (mother). He has a younger brother named Chris Kratt and twin sisters, Christine and Susan Kratt.

    Martin Kratt Wife | Is Martin Kratt Married

    He is married to Laura Wilkinson since 2000. The couple has two sons, Gavin and Ronan, both of whom have had roles on Wild Kratts.

    His son Gavin Kratt played himself in many episodes such as “Slider The Otter,” “Bass Class,” “Praying Mantis,” “Happy Turkey Day,” and many more. Ronan Kratt played himself in the same episodes as Gavin including “The Amazing Creature Race,” and “The Blue and the Grey.”

    Martin Kratt Net Worth

    He has an estimated net worth of $1 million.

    Martin Kratt Wild Kratts

    This show follows the adventures of animated versions of brothers Chris and Martin Kratt, hosts of such educational shows as “Kratts’ Creatures” and “Zoboomafoo.” In the show, the animated Kratts encounter wild animals during stories of adventure and mystery. The live-action Kratt brothers introduce each episode.

    Chris And Martin Kratt Zoboomafoo

    Martin and Chris Kratt star in this preschool series that teaches children how to respect and care for animals. Zoboomafoo, a playful little lemur, hangs around Animal Junction with Matt and Chris. The watering hole is an animal pit stop where a steady stream of animal visitors wander in from around the globe. The series uses music and sounds from around the world, puppetry, animation and Claymation to entertain kids.

    Martin Kratt Interview

     Martin Kratt interview about their new toy line!

    Interviewer: What made you decide to create your own Wild Kratts Toy Line?

    Martin Kratt: All of our shows have been about play, and Wild Kratts is specifically about playing with the creature powers of animals! So toys are a natural extension of the series.

    Interviewer: How did you decide what toys/animals you would make?

    Martin Kratt: This was the hardest part! We’ve featured so many animals in the series so far, and have activated lots of creature powers that are all really cool. We listened to our fans in the letters they wrote to us, about their favorite animals and creature power suits, and made our decisions based on what seemed to be the most popular!

    Interviewer: Did you help in designing the toys?

    Martin Kratt: Yes! We worked with our partners at Wicked Cool toys to come up with the ideas. And the 27 different toys in the stores now are just the beginning!  There are endless opportunities for fun, toys, and play in the Wild Kratts world.

    Interviewer: Do you have a favorite toy from the line?

    Martin Kratt: Right now – I’d have to say mine are the talking Martin and Chris plush. I find it hilarious that we, in our animated form, have now turned into stuffies!

    Interviewer: Do you have a favorite animal?

    Martin Kratt: That’s always the toughest question to answer! When I write scripts for Wild Kratts, I always learn something new about the featured animal. My favorite animal changes a lot and is usually the one I happen to be writing a Wild Kratts script about or filming.  So today, my favorite animal is a narwhal!

    Interviewer: What is the coolest animal you have worked with?

    Martin Kratt: That’s an impossible question for me to answer! We have worked with so many amazing animals. If had to choose the “coolest” animal, as in temperature and being cold, then it would have to be the Japanese Snow Monkey!

    Interviewer: What is the scariest animal you have worked with?

    Martin Kratt: That’s easy!  My brother when he can’t find his Creature Power Vest!
