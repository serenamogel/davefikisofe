    05 Feb 2024, 00:00

    You can’t just buy any condiments and call it a day. You need the best condiments. It’s like investing in a good capsule wardrobe—these are things you’ll be using every day, so you want them to be high quality. And, as a member of the Sporked team of taste testers, I can tell you with certainty that there are differences between yellow mustard brands and that not all sweet pickle relish is worthy of your hot dog. 

    So, what is the best condiment? After many, many, many taste tests in which we ate condiments straight from a spoon—as well as on foods—we pulled together all the best of the bests to make this list. Here are the best burger condiments, best hot dog condiments, and more top condiments that deserve a permanent place in your fridge and pantry.

    Best Horseradish Sauce

    Woeber’s Sandwich Pal Horseradish SauceAmazon

    Who doesn’t want a sandwich pal? Especially when it tastes this good. It’s creamy, zesty, and has a ton of horseradish kick. Feel free to use this however you see fit, but, if you eat red meat, do yourself a favor and try it on a roast beef sammie. This is the best condiment for a roast beef sandwich, for sure.

    Best Yellow Mustard

    Kroger Yellow MustardKroger

    Yes, a store-brand yellow mustard took the top spot in our yellow mustard taste test. In their review, senior staff writer Jordan Myrick wrote that it earned the title because it is the “mustardy mustard of the bunch.” It’s extremely tangy and tart—perfect for cutting through some sliced deli turkey and Swiss cheese.

    Best Dijon Mustard

    Maille Dijon OriginaleWalmart

    Not only will this Dijon mustard upgrade anything you slather it on, it will also add some class to your fridge. In their review, Jordan wrote that, of all the Dijons we tasted, Maille was the “punchiest [and] zestiest.” It will clear your nasal passages.

    Best Mayo

    Hellmann’s Real MayonnaiseWalmart

    Hellmann’s has stayed in the number one spot as best mayo over the course of two separate taste tests. It’s just that good. Sporked contributor Danny Palumbo wrote in his review that it is “triumphantly decadent” with “less acidic bite than Duke’s.” It’s just tart enough, but mostly it’s savory and slick. One of the best sandwich condiments, all around.

    Heinz Tomato KetchupWalmart

    After two separate taste tests of many, many ketchup brands, Heinz remains on the top of the list. It has a little more zip and sweetness to it than the other ketchup brands we tasted. It’s the top dog for a reason, and definitely one of the best burger condiments.

    Del Monte Sweet Pickle RelishRalphs

    This relish was so good it changed Jordan from a sweet relish skeptic into a sweet relish lover. The blend of cucumber, red peppers, onions, mustard seeds, and, of course, sugar, creates a delicious melange of flavor that is great on a burger or hot dog, or mixed into foods like deviled eggs.

    Best Hot Sauce

    Huy Fong Foods Sriracha Hot Chili SauceWalmart

    You know it. You love it. You might even think it’s the only sriracha out there (it’s not, there are many srirachas). And it’s a classic for a reason. It’s spicy—but not too spicy. It’s thick enough to be squirtable (a big plus when it comes to the best condiments). And, according to Jordan’s review, it’s good on anything, from scrambled eggs to avocado toast to pizza.

    Best Chili Crisp

    Natural Plus Green Sichuan Chili OilAmazon

    Over the past few years, chili crisp has gone from being a niche condiment to being the trendy, must-have condiment that people put on literally anything—including ice cream. Natural Plus’s version was so good, Jordan even said it was better than the stuff from their favorite dumpling restaurant. It’s numbing and packed with extra umami. And it’s definitely spicy.

    Silver Swan Special Soy SauceAmazon

    Our soy sauce taste test left the Sporked crew feeling particularly dehydrated, but it was all for the best because we discovered Silver Swan, a Filipino-made soy sauce. According to managing editor Gwynedd Stuart’s review, it “has an ultra-rich flavor, a hint of sweetness, and huge amounts of umami.” And it comes in a really big bottle so you don’t have to keep re-upping your stash.

It’s sweet, salty, and sour all at once. And it’s so very creamy—perfect for every morsel of flaky fried fish or crispy fried shrimp.

    Casa Sanchez Foods Hot Salsa RojaAmazon

The company manages to do so much with just a few simple ingredients: tomatoes, onions, peppers, and cilantro. That’s all it takes!

    Best Pesto

    Kirkland Basil PestoCostco

    This is not only the best pesto, it’s also one of the best things to buy at Costco, in general. It has a bit of a cult following and for good reason. It’s herby, and cheesy, and nutty, and not too garlicky. Plus, the olive oil tastes like high quality olive oil. Look for it in the refrigerated section!

    Best Sweet Chili Sauce

    Pantai Sweetened Chili Sauce for Spring RollWalmart

    While I still think you should save those packets from your local Chinese takeout rather than throwing them away, you should also get a bottle of this sweet chili sauce for things like frozen egg rolls or dumplings or pretty much anything fried. You can see the pepper bits floating in the sauce, but don’t worry, it’s not spicy at all. It’s sweet and mildly tangy and just as good as (if not better than) the stuff from the takeout spot.
