    Ryland Peter “Ry” Cooder (born March 15, 1947) is a musician from the United States of America. Aside from his slide guitar work, he is renowned for his interest in roots music from the United States and, more recently, his collaborations with traditional performers from a variety of cultures. Throughout his career, his solo music has been diverse, spanning folk, blues, Tex-Mex, soul, gospel, rock, and a variety of other genres. In addition to Captain Beefheart and Ali Farka Touré, Eric Clapton and The Rolling Stones, Van Morrison and Neil Young, as well as David Lindley and The Chieftains, he has worked with a number of other artists, most notably The Chieftains and The Doobie Brothers.

     He temporarily established a band called Little Village, which disbanded after a few months. A global smash album, the Buena Vista Social Club’s Buena Vista Social Club (1997), was produced by Ry Cooder. Wim Wenders directed the documentary film of the same name (which was nominated for an Academy Award in 2000), which was directed by the same name. He was rated ninth on Rolling Stone magazine’s list of “The 100 Greatest Guitarists of All Time” in 2003, which was published in 200Gibson ranked him 32nd in the world in a rating published in 2010.
